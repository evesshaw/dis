THE PROCESS

Portrayals of Asians in Film

Visualising Ithaca College Library Data: Graphs, Illustrations and Interactive Database Exploring

It all started with my love for films, and teenage obsession with Lindsay Lohan.

Freaky Friday was a movie I've watched over and over again growing up. It was the reason I wanted to form a band, to dye my hair, and to perform at the House of Blues. More than a decade has passed since it came out, but it will forever hold a special place in my heart. Perhaps it wasn't a perfect 10 to critics, but everything about it was to me - aside from the fact that the Chinese restaruant owners spoke okay Mandarin and Cantonese, but many of the sentences made no sense.

To a young me, that error was just some confusion and a good laugh. However, as I've grown older, I've realised that being surprised every time I see someone that resembles me appear on the screen should not have been a normal and common reaction among my community. Aside from a quick surpsised reaction to seeing some representation in Western media, I've also realised that we are more often than not, an insignificant role, including the sidekick, the best friend, or someone stereotypical, and someone, the most amusing of all, supposedly fluent in an Asian language but even native speakers fail to make out what they're saying.

To look into how Asians are portrayed in film and bring an understanding to the topic, I've decided to work with the curated list of films from the library of Ithaca College. After cleaning and clearing the data I've gathered from them into an Excel worksheet, I've produced three data visualisations using D3.js.

Ithaca College is ranked 17th in the Top 50 best film schools in the US, which means that its collection isn't too minimal. Just by clicking through their collection and selecting the 'Action and Adventures Film' section, 586 results showed up. However, as seen in the bar chart above, the decade with the most films of Asian portrayals is the 00's, yet there aren't even 50 films during that period in their collection - 38 to be exact, and the total amount showcasing Asian portrayals? 133, including content containing Yellowface, non-speaking roles or a simple mention of the community.

Only four genres exist in the 'Portrayals of Asians in Film' collection in the Ithaca College Library: Drama, Film Adaptions, Action and Juvenielle Film. Upon clearing the data, I've noticed that Asians are often protrayed as villans, martial artists, or as a 'bond girl' when featured in Action films.

Asian women, if they are 'good', they are seen as submissive and serving, but if they are loud and aggressive, then they are the hot 'dragon lady', and either way, labeled, stereotyped and sexualised.

Asian men, on the other hand, face the opposite problem. Still labeled and often stereotypical, but they are viewed awkward, effeminate, nerdy, tech-savvy, and so on. The labels are endless, but most of it are catorgorised as indesirable and odd in a negative way, enforcing an image that Asian actors are desperately to break out of.
