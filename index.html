THE PROCESS

Portrayals of Asians in Film

A visual representation of the Ithaca College Library database

Although my experience as someone born and raised in Asia is ultimately different than those that grew up in areas where Asians are not of dominance, I’ve seen as many Hollywood-produced films as most, and I was as surprised as anyone every time I see someone that resembles me appear on the screen. Despite the fact that they are more often than not the sidekick, the best friend, or someone stereotypical, highly sexualised, and most amusing of all, who supposedly spoke my language but made no sense in whatever they’re saying, it was always exciting to see some form of representation on screen.

As seen in the graph above, the amount of films in the ICL collection with Asian portrayals is incredibly minimal. A trivial thing to be happy about is that at least there were representations pre-WWII, and that there were opportunities for Asians in films. But is that really the case? Grab your tickets and let's take a closer look!

As seen in the projector above, there are 4 films (with valid links) available in the collection of ICL for the period of Pre WWII. Since there are only four films, let's take a closer look at each one.

Let's begin with The Cheat. In the 1915 version, the film stars a wealthy Japanese villain (and actually portrayed by a Japanese-American). However, when re-released in 1918, the character somehow became Burmese, perhaps unintentionally reaffirming the weird, confusing and offensive belief 'Asians are all the sameIn' belief some seem to have. In Broken Blossoms. we catch one of the earliest examples of Yellowface, as Richard Barthelmess, a Caucasian actor, portrays a Chinese man who befriends an abused English girl.

A case of Yellowface happens yet again in The Good Earth. Paul Muni and Luise Rainer portrayed a Chinese couple, despite the fact that the author Pearl Buck asked for the film to be casted with Chinese or Chinese-American actors. In Hollywood (2020), writer Ryan Murphy reimagined the era and Michelle Krusiec protrayed Anna May Wong, who did get to star in The Good Earth, and win an Oscar for the role.

Last but not least, Lost Horizon, is a fantasy film about Shangri-La in the Himilayas. However, there’s nary an Asian actor in the cast.
